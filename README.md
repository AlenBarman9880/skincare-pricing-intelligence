# ecommerce-pricing-intelligence
End-to-end competitive intelligence and pricing strategy engine for skincare brands using web scraping, analytics, KPI tracking, and pricing recommendations.

## Week 1 Deliverable – Competitive Landscape Mapping (Skincare Domain)
### 1. Project Overview

This project aims to build a competitive intelligence and pricing strategy engine for the skincare market by analyzing competitor pricing behavior, discount strategies, bundling patterns, and brand positioning across major e-commerce platforms.

### 2. Problem Statement

In the skincare industry, brands compete not only on product quality but also on:

- Pricing strategy
- Promotional discounts
- Product bundling
- Consumer trust and reviews
- Platform-specific positioning

Without structured competitor intelligence, brands may fail to optimize pricing, promotions, and product positioning.

### 6. Pricing Tier Analysis

Based on observed pricing:

| Tier      | Price Range |
| --------- | ----------- |
| Budget    | ₹200–₹400   |
| Mid-Range | ₹400–₹800   |
| Premium   | ₹800+       |

##### Observations
- Mamaearth primarily targets the budget-to-mid segment.
- Minimalist focuses on performance-driven mid-range pricing.
- Cetaphil maintains premium pricing with low discount dependency.

### 7. Discount Pattern Analysis

Observed discount behavior:

- D2C brands frequently offer discounts between 10%–25%
- Legacy brands show more stable pricing
- Platform-exclusive offers influence final selling price

##### Example Insights
- Mamaearth uses aggressive promotional pricing.
- Neutrogena shows lower discount frequency but stronger trust positioning.

### 8. Bundling Strategy Analysis

Observed bundling strategies:

##### Common Bundle Types
- Face Wash + Moisturizer
- Serum + Sunscreen
- Trial kits / combo packs

##### Key Insight
D2C brands rely more heavily on bundling to increase basket size compared to established pharmaceutical/dermatological brands.

### 10. Competitive Positioning

Based on market observations, the proposed company strategy is:
| Position as a mid-premium skincare brand offering science-backed formulations at competitive prices, supported by selective discounting and high-value bundles.

##### Strategic Differentiators
- Higher ingredient transparency
- Controlled discounting
- Review-driven trust positioning
- Value-based bundling

### 11. Initial Strategic Opportunities

Based on competitor analysis:

##### Opportunity 1
Premium competitors show weak bundling adoption.

##### Opportunity 2
Aggressive discounting is concentrated among D2C brands.

##### Opportunity 3
Cross-platform pricing inconsistency creates arbitrage opportunities.
